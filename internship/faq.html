1. When does the internship process strats?

The internship process starts from august to the end of the year. many company visits campus and interviews are done. Students are selected for the internships. For any details write to placement@nitk.ac.in.

2. What does the internship process comprise of?

The internship process involves:

  • Communication of interest in the placement program to the placement officer
  • Finalizing an internship slot
  • Pre placement Presentation
  • Shortlisting of students based on company's basic eligibility criteria
  • Written tests/group discussions
  • Interviews
  • Selection list

3.How can a company register itself for the Campus Recruitment Program?

To participate in the Campus Recruitment Program, a company must first contact the placement office or register on the website. The company is then sent the login details which would let them login in our Placement Automation System (PAS) enabling them to post job opportunities & see the profiles of students interested in their job opening. You can also find the detailed internship Procedure on website.

4. How do I obtain a placement brochure?

The placement brochure for the year can be downloaded from the website internships.nitk.ac.in. For a printed copy of the placement brochure kindly e-mail your request at placement@nitk.ac.in. Student profiles can also be obtained on request to the same.

5. What time of the year is the summer internship?

The academic schedule for the summer internship is normally between the second-week of May and mid-June. The internship may last for a minimum of 8 weeks to a maximum of 10 weeks. For final year students the summer internship is between Feb and May and may last for a minimum of 12 weeks to a maximum of 14 weeks.

6. Will it be possible to conduct the placement processes off-campus?

Conduction of off-campus internship processes may be considered on a case-to-case basis. Please consult the placement officer for working out the details. The company will have to, however, make the Corporate Presentation on-campus.

7. What is the procedure to schedule a pre-placement talk (PPT)?

A company will only be allowed to hold its PPT after filling up the job announcement form. A typical pre placement session is scheduled for 60 minutes. In view of the academic calendar of the students, the session are scheduled between 6PM to 9PM on weekdays, and between 10AM and 5PM hours on Saturdays and Sundays. Student volunteers will get in touch with the company to fix a mutual convenient time for the session.

8. What kind of information do the students expect in PPTs?

The pre-placement talk is a platform for interaction between the company and the students. The presentation is followed by a Q&A session where students interact with company officials to get a better understanding of the company and potential roles. The important insights that the students expect from the PPT are:

  • Profile and reputation of the corporation
  • Career roles and responsibilities offered in different types of profiles
  • Locations where the student is likely to be posted
  • Compensation packages

9. On what basis is the slot allotted to a company for the final placement?

Slotting is done subject to the following parameters:

  • Student Preferences
  • Work profile
  • Compensation package
  • Career Prospects
  • Student Intake
  • Past relationship with IIT Kanpur

10. Is there any facility for the accommodation available on the campus?

On campus accommodation on payment basis in visitors' guest house is available subject to availability. For alternate arrangements, the list of hotels is available.

11. Can a company interview the students already placed in other company?

NIT Karnataka follows a One student; One job policy so once the job is registered against the student, he/she is not entitled to sit for any other company.

12. Are there multiple companies recruiting in the same slot? How is the case of multiple offers resolved?

Yes, there are multiple companies recruiting in the same slot.The results of all the companies are announced at the end of the slot and if a student gets multiple offers then he is allowed to choose between one of them and inform the office of his decision before the beginning of the next slot.
